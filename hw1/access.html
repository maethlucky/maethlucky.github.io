Access control is the process of regulating who can view, use, or modify resources in a computer system. It helps protect sensitive data by ensuring that only authorized users are granted appropriate permissions based on identity, role, or policy.

Types of Access Control:

  • Discretionary (DAC)
  • Mandatory (MAC)
  • Role-Based (RBAC)
  • Rule-Based
  • Attribute-Based (ABAC)
  • Access Control Matrix (ACM)
